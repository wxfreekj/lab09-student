üìö Introduction

Weather forecasting involves predicting future weather by analyzing current and recent atmospheric conditions. To make accurate forecasts, meteorologists use various charts and images:

  • 500 hPa Charts: These show conditions around 18,000 feet, indicating upper-level wind patterns and pressure systems.
  • 925 hPa Charts: These represent lower atmosphere conditions, indicating temperature and wind near Earth's surface.
  • Satellite/Radar and Surface Observations: Provide visual information on cloud cover, precipitation, and the position of fronts.

üéØ Step-by-Step Instructions for Forecasting

Use these interactive image viewers to examine the meteorological charts. Click "Next" and "Previous" to navigate through each set of images. Click on an image or the Zoom button to view it full-screen.

Step 1: Analyze 500 hPa Charts

  • Look for troughs (dips indicating potential stormy weather) and ridges (bumps indicating calmer weather)
  • Identify the general direction and movement of these features

Step 2: Analyze 925 hPa Charts

  • Note temperature patterns and areas of warmer or colder air
  • Identify wind direction and speed at lower levels

Step 3: Review Surface Observations and Satellite/Radar Images

  • Locate fronts (warm, cold, stationary, occluded) and their direction of movement
  • Examine cloud coverage, precipitation, and storms visible on satellite and radar

Step 4: Combine Observations to Create Your Forecast

  • Predict temperature changes based on air movement and frontal passages
  • Determine wind direction changes based on chart data
  • Assess precipitation likelihood or severe weather potential by integrating satellite/radar imagery with surface conditions

Forecast for Virginia:

  • Temperature (F): Low 60s, possibly upper 50s due to rain
  • Wind Direction: Southerly
  • Precipitation/Severe Weather: Rain likely; severe weather unlikely

Explanation: Warm, moist air moving northward due to an approaching upper-level system and developing surface low will increase temperatures into the 60s. However, the expected rain might keep temperatures slightly cooler, in the upper 50s. Sufficient warmth and low-level moisture are needed for severe weather development.
